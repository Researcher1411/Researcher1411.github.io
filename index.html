About the Researcher

Hi! I am a law graduate with a deep passion for true crime and a keen eye for detail. I have always been drawn to the complexities of real-life cases — the timelines, the evidence, the psychology behind the crime — and I love diving into the research that brings these stories to life.

Whether you're a content creator, podcaster, or filmmaker, I can help you find accurate information, build solid case timelines, and dig up the kind of lesser-known details that make a story really compelling. I enjoy the process of piecing everything together and making sure no fact goes unchecked..

If you're looking for someone who is genuinely invested in the subject and easy to work with, I will be happy to collaborate!

Research Methodology

My approach combines legal analysis, psychological profiling, and historical context to provide comprehensive case studies.

1

Case Selection

Identifying cases with historical significance, unresolved questions, or psychological complexity that warrant deeper examination.

2

Archival Research

Gathering court documents, police reports, news archives, and any available primary sources related to the case.

3

Forensic Analysis

Examining crime scene details, autopsy reports, and forensic evidence with attention to investigative procedures.

4

Psychological Profiling

Developing psychological profiles of perpetrators and victims based on behavioral evidence and expert literature.

5

Legal Review

Analyzing trial proceedings, legal strategies, and judicial decisions through a critical legal lens.

6

Contextualization

Placing the case within its historical, social, and cultural context to understand broader implications.
